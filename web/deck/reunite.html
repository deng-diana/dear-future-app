The person you are right now — your fears, who you love today, the way your voice shakes — will be a stranger to you in ten years.

And you will have no way to meet them again.

We record everything.
We remember nothing.

  • 1.8 trillion photos taken a year — in camera rolls we never reopen.
  • Journals abandoned by January.
  • Nothing we capture is addressed to the future.

Memory has never been so recorded — and so lost.

A time capsule isn’t a scheduled email.

FutureMe and reminder apps send text on a delay. They schedule. They nag about tomorrow.

None of them carry the real you to a future day — and all of them fight to be opened today.

✦

What if an app succeeded by how completely you forgot it?

The first product with an inverted KPI: the better it works, the less you think about it — until, one day, it finds you.

What Reunite is

  • Write a letter to your future self — with a photo and a short video.
  • Choose a day at least fifteen days out.
  • Seal it.
  • The moment you seal, it vanishes.
  • On that future day, it returns as an email — a reunion with the person you used to be.
✦

Sealing is disappearing.

No list. No inbox. No badge. No “in transit.”
The app never shows you a letter you wrote.

A vault you could open later is just a list in disguise. We refuse it.

Aligned by design

One charge at sealing — and it prepays that capsule’s storage. Revenue and cost are aligned by design: no ongoing liability, no ads, no attention economy.

Cents to keep, worth dollars

Storing one sealed letter and its media for ten years costs cents. People gladly pay a few dollars for a sacred moment — a bottle of aged wine, a gravestone, a wedding. We charge there.

Upside: longer video, multiple capsules, gifting, and “deliver to someone else” — a letter to your child, arriving on their 18th birthday.
