Reunite is a digital time capsule. You write a letter to your future self, choose a day at least fifteen days away, and seal it. On that day, the letter comes back to you as an email. This page explains, in plain language, what information we hold, why, and what control you have over it.

We have tried to write this the way the app itself is built: quietly, honestly, and without tricks. Where we cannot make a promise, we say so plainly — including the important point that your letters are not end-to-end encrypted (see “An honest note about encryption” below).

An honest note about encryption

This is the one point we most want to be straight about.

Your letter is encrypted in transit (while it travels over the internet) and encrypted at rest (while it sits in our storage). But your letter is not end-to-end encrypted. End-to-end encryption would mean even we could never read it. We cannot honestly claim that — because the whole point of Reunite is that, on your chosen day, our system must read your letter to email it back to you.

We promise this in return: we access stored letters only to operate and protect the service (for example, to deliver them or fix a delivery problem). We never read your letters to advertise, profile, train models, or sell. We keep staff access tightly limited. We would rather tell you this plainly than dress it up as something it isn’t.

How long we keep your information

  • A sealed, undelivered letter is stored until its delivery date, then emailed to you and marked as delivered.
  • After delivery, the full text of your letter also lives in your own email inbox — an inbox you control, independent of us. This is intentional: your capsule should not depend only on our servers surviving.
  • Your account information (your email address) is kept while your account exists.

Account deletion and your choices

You can delete your account at any time, from inside the app (and via a web deletion page add the public web deletion URL once built).

Please read this carefully, because it cannot be undone: deleting your account permanently cancels every letter you have sealed but not yet received. Those letters will never be delivered. Deletion also removes your stored account data and letter content from our systems. (Letters already delivered remain in your own email inbox, because that copy belongs to you.)
